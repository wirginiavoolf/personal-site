Phantoms


Every time I get on the bus, I fall asleep
and wake up to the nightmare of your face.
On the street, I look for apparitions
in the dark tresses of twinning bodies.

A chance meeting emerges when waves pull
garbage out of the water and onto the shore.
Soda can rings croak through a fish's neck.

I said no when you asked to marry me
in my sleep, so instead you made me the ring
you slipped on and off until I woke up screaming.

What was thrown on top of me didn't fill my lungs
with water, but sometimes just the waves
are like a phantom limb. Even years later,
I can feel their arms crashing against my hips.
chalk erasers cymbaling together in clouds of unrained skies.





Originally published in Moonsick Magazine.
